# Anti-AI Style Guide: Signs of AI Writing to AVOID

> **Source:** [Wikipedia: Signs of AI Writing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Signs_of_AI_writing)
>
> **Purpose:** Read this BEFORE writing any content for GeorgiaGAPP.com. Every pattern listed here is something to actively avoid. This is our quality firewall against AI-sounding content.

---

## How to Use This Guide

This is a **"DO NOT DO"** list. Before writing any blog post, county page, content page, or marketing copy for GeorgiaGAPP.com, scan your output against these patterns. If you see them, rewrite.

---

## 1. Content Patterns to Avoid

### 1.1 Undue Emphasis on Significance, Legacy, and Broader Trends

**BANNED phrases:**
- "stands/serves as"
- "is a testament/reminder"
- "a vital/significant/crucial/pivotal/key role/moment"
- "underscores/highlights its importance/significance"
- "reflects broader"
- "symbolizing its ongoing/enduring/lasting"
- "contributing to the"
- "setting the stage for"
- "marking/shaping the"
- "represents/marks a shift"
- "key turning point"
- "evolving landscape"
- "focal point"
- "indelible mark"
- "deeply rooted"

**What this looks like (BAD):**
> "The founding of the GAPP program represented a significant shift toward regional healthcare independence, enabling Georgia to develop a system tailored to its unique needs. This initiative was part of a broader movement to enhance pediatric care."

**What to do instead:**
State facts plainly. Don't inflate importance. Let the reader decide significance.

> "Georgia created the GAPP program to get home nursing care to kids who need it. Here's how it works."

### 1.2 Undue Emphasis on Notability and Attribution

**BANNED phrases:**
- "independent coverage"
- "local/regional/national media outlets"
- "profiled in"
- "written by a leading expert"
- "active social media presence"
- "maintains a strong digital presence"

**What this looks like (BAD):**
> "GeorgiaGAPP.com has been recognized by healthcare professionals and family advocates as a trusted resource for connecting families with verified GAPP providers."

**What to do instead:**
Don't assert your own importance. Just be useful and let the work speak.

### 1.3 Superficial Analyses

**BANNED phrases:**
- "highlighting/underscoring/emphasizing ..."
- "ensuring ..."
- "reflecting/symbolizing ..."
- "contributing to ..."
- "cultivating/fostering ..." (figurative)
- "encompassing ..."
- "valuable insights"
- "align/resonate with"

AI loves to attach "-ing" phrases to the end of sentences as fake analysis.

**What this looks like (BAD):**
> "The directory helps families find providers in their county, ensuring access to quality care while fostering connections between caregivers and the families they serve."

**What to do instead:**
Cut the "-ing" clause. End the sentence earlier.

> "The directory helps families find providers in their county."

### 1.4 Promotional and Advertisement-Like Language

**BANNED phrases:**
- "boasts a"
- "vibrant"
- "rich" (figurative)
- "profound"
- "enhancing"
- "showcasing"
- "exemplifies"
- "commitment to"
- "natural beauty"
- "nestled"
- "in the heart of"
- "groundbreaking" (figurative)
- "renowned"
- "featuring"
- "diverse array"

**What this looks like (BAD):**
> "Nestled in the heart of Metro Atlanta, Fulton County boasts a vibrant network of GAPP providers showcasing Georgia's commitment to pediatric home care excellence."

**What to do instead:**
> "Fulton County has 47 GAPP providers. Most are in Atlanta and Sandy Springs."

### 1.5 Vague Attributions and Overgeneralization

**BANNED phrases:**
- "Industry reports"
- "Observers have cited"
- "Experts argue"
- "Some critics argue"
- "several sources/publications" (when only few are cited)

**Rule:** If you can't name the expert, don't cite "experts." If you can't link the report, don't reference "reports."

### 1.6 Outline-Like "Challenges and Future Prospects" Conclusions

**BANNED pattern:** "Despite its [positive words], [subject] faces challenges..."

**What this looks like (BAD):**
> "Despite its comprehensive network of providers, Georgia's GAPP program faces challenges including long approval timelines and limited rural coverage. Despite these challenges, ongoing improvements continue to enhance access for families across the state."

**What to do instead:**
If there are real problems, state them directly without the "despite" sandwich.

> "Approval takes 4-8 weeks. Rural counties have fewer providers. Here's how to work around both."

## 2. Language and Grammar Patterns to Avoid

### 2.1 High-Density "AI Vocabulary" Words

These words are statistically overused by AI. One or two is fine. A cluster of them screams AI.

**Words to watch:**
- Additionally (especially starting a sentence)
- align with
- boasts (meaning "has")
- bolstered
- crucial
- delve
- emphasizing
- enduring
- enhance
- fostering
- garner
- highlight (as a verb)
- interplay
- intricate/intricacies
- key (as adjective)
- landscape (abstract noun)
- meticulous/meticulously
- pivotal
- showcase
- tapestry (abstract noun)
- testament
- underscore (as verb)
- valuable
- vibrant

**Era breakdown (what clusters together):**
- **2023-mid 2024:** Additionally, boasts, bolstered, crucial, delve, emphasizing, enduring, garner, intricate, interplay, key, landscape, meticulous, pivotal, underscore, tapestry, testament, valuable, vibrant
- **Mid 2024-mid 2025:** align with, bolstered, crucial, emphasizing, enhance, enduring, fostering, highlighting, pivotal, showcasing, underscore, vibrant
- **Mid 2025+:** emphasizing, enhance, highlighting, showcasing

**Self-test:** After writing a paragraph, count how many of these words appear. More than 1 per paragraph = rewrite.

### 2.2 Avoidance of Simple "Is/Are" Phrases

AI replaces simple verbs with fancier alternatives.

**BAD:** "The directory serves as a resource for families"
**GOOD:** "The directory is a resource for families"

**BAD:** "Fulton County features four major providers"
**GOOD:** "Fulton County has four major providers"

**BAD:** "The quiz offers a personalized matching experience"
**GOOD:** "The quiz matches you with providers"

### 2.3 Negative Parallelisms

**BANNED patterns:**
- "Not only ... but also ..."
- "It's not just about ..., it's ..."
- "Not X, but Y"
- "No ..., no ..., just ..."

**What this looks like (BAD):**
> "GAPP isn't just a program -- it's a lifeline for families navigating the complex world of pediatric home care."

**What to do instead:**
> "GAPP pays for home nursing so your kid doesn't have to stay in the hospital."

### 2.4 Rule of Three

AI overuses three-item lists for fake comprehensiveness: "adjective, adjective, and adjective" or "short phrase, short phrase, and short phrase."

**What this looks like (BAD):**
> "The GAPP program provides comprehensive, compassionate, and coordinated care for medically fragile children."

**What to do instead:**
Pick the one that matters most, or use two if both matter.

> "The GAPP program pays for home nursing for medically fragile kids."

### 2.5 Elegant Variation

AI avoids repeating words by swapping in synonyms excessively. If you're talking about "providers," keep calling them "providers." Don't rotate through "agencies," "care partners," "service organizations," and "healthcare entities."

**Rule:** Repeat the plain word. Readers prefer clarity over variety.

### 2.6 Overuse of Em Dashes

AI uses em dashes (--) where commas, parentheses, or colons work better. One em dash per article is fine. Three or more = rewrite.

## 3. Style Patterns to Avoid

### 3.1 Title Case in Headings

**BAD:** "How To Find The Right GAPP Provider For Your Family"
**GOOD:** "How to find the right GAPP provider for your family"

Use sentence case for headings unless it's a proper noun.

### 3.2 Overuse of Boldface

Don't bold every **key term** in a paragraph. Bold is for headings and occasionally for one critical word. If more than 10% of a paragraph is bold, strip it back.

### 3.3 Inline-Header Vertical Lists

**BAD:**
- **Comprehensive Coverage:** We list providers across all 159 Georgia counties
- **Verified Quality:** Every provider is checked for licensing
- **Easy Access:** Find providers with our simple search tool

**GOOD:**
We list providers in all 159 Georgia counties. Every provider is checked for licensing. Search by county or service type.

(Prose is almost always better than decorated bullet lists.)

## 4. Structural Red Flags

### 4.1 "Challenges and Future Prospects" Sections

Never end an article with a section about challenges followed by optimistic future outlook. This is the #1 structural tell of AI content.

### 4.2 Section Summaries and Conclusions

**BANNED opening words for final paragraphs:**
- "In summary"
- "In conclusion"
- "Overall"

End with something useful -- a phone number, a next step, a link. Not a recap.

### 4.3 Didactic Disclaimers

**BANNED phrases:**
- "It's important/critical/crucial to note/remember/consider"
- "Worth noting"
- "May vary"

Don't tell readers what's important. Just state the fact.

**BAD:** "It's important to note that GAPP eligibility requirements may vary depending on your child's specific medical condition."

**GOOD:** "Eligibility depends on your child's medical condition. Here's what qualifies."

## 5. The Replacement Playbook

When you catch yourself writing AI patterns, use these replacements:

| AI Pattern | Human Replacement |
|---|---|
| "serves as a vital resource" | "helps families find..." |
| "plays a crucial role in" | "matters because..." or just cut it |
| "reflects a broader trend" | (delete the sentence) |
| "showcasing their commitment to" | (delete the sentence) |
| "it's important to note that" | (just state the fact) |
| "comprehensive and compassionate" | pick one, or neither |
| "diverse array of" | "several" or "many" |
| "in the heart of" | "in" |
| "nestled in" | "in" |
| "boasts" | "has" |
| "enhancing" | "improving" or cut it |
| "fostering" | "building" or "creating" |
| "leveraging" | "using" |
| "landscape" (abstract) | "field" or "world" or cut it |
| "tapestry" (abstract) | (delete the sentence) |
| "Not only X but also Y" | state X. Then state Y. Two sentences. |

## 6. The Voice We Want

For GeorgiaGAPP.com, write like:

- **A knowledgeable friend** who's been through the GAPP process, not a healthcare textbook
- **Direct and specific** -- county names, phone numbers, timelines, not generalities
- **Short sentences** -- if a sentence has a comma followed by an "-ing" word, split it or cut the second half
- **Real talk** -- "This takes 4-8 weeks" not "The approval process encompasses a comprehensive timeline"
- **Parent-to-parent** -- "Your kid" not "your medically fragile child" (except where clinical precision matters)
- **Action-oriented** -- every paragraph should answer "what do I do next?"

### Before/After Examples for GAPP Content

**BAD (AI-sounding):**
> "Finding a qualified GAPP provider can be a daunting task for families navigating the complex landscape of pediatric home care in Georgia. Our comprehensive directory serves as a vital resource, showcasing verified providers across all 159 counties, ensuring that families can connect with trusted caregivers who align with their unique needs."

**GOOD (human-sounding):**
> "You need a GAPP provider. We have 327 listed across Georgia. Search by your county, pick a service type (RN, LPN, or personal care), and request a callback. Most families hear back within 48 hours."

## 7. Pre-Publish Checklist

Before publishing any content, verify:

- [ ] No more than 1 "AI vocabulary" word per paragraph
- [ ] No "-ing" phrases tacked onto sentence ends as fake analysis
- [ ] No "serves as" / "stands as" -- use "is"
- [ ] No "despite its challenges" sandwich structure
- [ ] No "it's important to note" disclaimers
- [ ] No "not only X but also Y" constructions
- [ ] No clusters of three adjectives or three parallel phrases
- [ ] No abstract uses of "landscape," "tapestry," "testament"
- [ ] Em dashes used sparingly (max 1-2 per article)
- [ ] Headings in sentence case, not Title Case
- [ ] Bold used only for headings, not for emphasis throughout
- [ ] Final paragraph gives a next step, not a summary
- [ ] Could a stressed parent scan this in 30 seconds and get what they need?
